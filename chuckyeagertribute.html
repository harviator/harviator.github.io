Brigadier General Charles E. "Chuck" Yeager

The man who broke the sound barrier

Some Highlights From General Yeager's Life

  • 1923 - Born Febrauary 13th, 1923 in West Virgina
  • 1941 - Enlisted in the Army Air Force
  • 1943 - Received his pilot wings and was shipped overseas to England
  • 1944 - Was shot down, returned to England, and made "Ace In A Day" shooting down 5 enemy aircraft on a single mission
  • 1945 - Continued to serve in the Air Force as a test pilot
  • 1947 - Became the fastest man alive breaking the sound barrier with two broken ribs
  • 1975 - After other achievements, records, and miliary commands Yeager retired from the Air Force
  • 2020 - General Yeager passed away at 97 in Los Angeles hospital
